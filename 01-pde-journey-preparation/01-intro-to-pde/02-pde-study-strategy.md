# Building Your Study Strategy — Course Setup Explained

## Course Goal
**Mr. X:** What is the main goal of this course for me?  
**Mr. Artificial King:** The primary goal is to help you **create a focused study strategy**. Instead of studying everything randomly, you learn **which areas you need to work on most**.

## How the Course Is Organized
**Mr. X:** How is the course structured?  
**Mr. Artificial King:** The course is organized around the **exam guide sections**.  
- Each module focuses on **one section of the exam guide**
- Your **course workbook** follows the same structure
- This makes it easy to connect learning with exam objectives

## Diagnostic Questions
**Mr. X:** What are diagnostic questions and why are they important?  
**Mr. Artificial King:** Diagnostic questions help you **check your current understanding**.  
- You answer them in **each module**
- Many questions use the **Cymbal Retail scenario**
- They ask you to **apply real Professional Data Engineer concepts**

You can answer these questions:
- Through an **on-demand quiz**
- Or directly in your **workbook**

## No Pressure to Be Perfect
**Mr. X:** Do I need to get all the answers right?  
**Mr. Artificial King:** Not at all.  
- These questions are **not the full exam**
- They are designed to **find knowledge gaps**
- This course is meant for the **early stage of your preparation**
- It’s okay if you’re **not an expert yet**

If something feels confusing, you should **ask your instructor**.

## Reviewing Answers & Learning What to Study
**Mr. X:** What happens after I answer the questions?  
**Mr. Artificial King:** After completing the questions, you:
- Review the **correct answers**
- Identify **what you need to study more**
- Learn **where to find the right resources**

The questions include **bolded hints** that show:
- What details are important
- How to evaluate answer choices

## Tips for Multiple-Choice Questions
**Mr. X:** Any advice for answering multiple-choice questions?  
**Mr. Artificial King:** Yes, remember these tips:
- Read the **question stem carefully**
- Make sure you understand **exactly what is being asked**
- Try to **predict the answer before looking at options**
- Sometimes **more than one answer may seem correct**
- Take questions **at face value**
- If details are missing, they likely **don’t matter**
- Watch for qualifiers like:
  - *usually, all, never, none*
  - *best, least, except*
